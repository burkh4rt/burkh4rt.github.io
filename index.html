About Me

I'm a recently-graduated Ph.D. from Brown University's Division of Applied Mathematics. For my dissertation, I developed a novel approach to Bayesian filtering, the Discriminative Kalman Filter. We successfully implemented this filter as part of the BrainGate Clinical Trial to enable participants with quadriplegia to communicate and interact with their environments in real time using mental imagery alone.
